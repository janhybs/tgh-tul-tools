ELECTRIC Electricity network

Consider simplified model of an electricity network consisting of $P$ powerplants, $A$ appliances, and $N$ junktions (we call any of these a node). These nodes are connected by a network of $M$ wires with prescribed maximal throughput that is same in both directions. No wire connects two powerplants directly. For every appliance, we know its consumption and we assume zero looses on wires and junktions. The goal is decide if the network is sufficient to cover consumption of appliances and if so, determine the production of every powerplant to cover the comsumption. If there are two possible vectors $a$ and $b$ of powerplant prodictions we prefere that with comes later in lexicographic ordering, that is have biger the first different value.

Input

There are four numbers on the first line giving number of powerplants $P$, number of appliances $A$, number of junktions $N$ and number of wires $M$. Nodes are indexed from zero numbering first powerplants, then appliances, and finally junktions. On following $A$ lines there are comsumptions of appliances. On following $M$ lines there is description of wires givin three numbers per line including index of the start node, index of the target node, and the capacity of a wire.

Output

The output consists of the $P$ lines giving production of every powerplant.

Example


Input:
2 2 1 4
4
6
0 4 6 
1 4 5
4 2 4
4 3 7

Output:
6
5
