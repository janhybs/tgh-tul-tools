BIGLOKO1 Big Loko

Big Loko company plans to connect N towns by the railroad. A study was made to evaluate some conections betwean pairs of towns. For some pairs, multiple variants were evaluated. Total number of evaluated connections was M. The study has also evaluated impact on the environment by marks 1 up to 5. The company wants a program, that designs two railroad networks: one with minimal total cost and one with minimal environmental impact and then with minimal cost.

Input

There are two numbers N and M on the first line. N is number of towns, M is number of evaluated connections. On following M lines, there are results of the study for individual connections. For every connections, there are: four integers, first two are indices of connected towns (0.. N-1), the third is evaluated cost (in milions), and the fourth is the environment mark.

Output

On the first N-1 lines, there are indices of connections used for the cheapest network (indexed from 0 to M-1). Consider the resulting spanning trees rooted in the town 0 and output the connection in such order that on line J there will be connection from the town J+1 to its predecesstor in the rooted tree. Line N is empty, folowed by N-1 lines with indices of the nature friendly network.

Example


Input:
4 7
0 1 20  5
0 1 40  2
1 2 5   3
1 3 11  2
1 3 20  1
0 2 40  2 
2 3 30  4

Output:
0
2
3

1
5
4
